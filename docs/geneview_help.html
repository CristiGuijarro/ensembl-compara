Ensembl GeneView

Ensembl 'GeneView' provides detailed information about automatically annotated Ensembl, manually curated Vega or transient EST gene model predictions. Ab initio predicted GENSCAN and SNAP transcripts are not clustered into genes and have therefore no corresponding 'Gene Reports'.

Ensembl Gene Report

The 'Ensembl Gene Report' at the top of the page provides the following data about the gene. Note: For manually curated Vega genes or for EST genes, not all of this information is displayed.

  • Gene - the name of the gene, obtained by comparing the gene's transcripts to entries in UniProt/Swiss-Prot, NCBI RefSeq and UniProt/TrEMBL. For further details see the 'Similarity Matches' section of the 'Transcript/Translation Summary' below. A single name is selected for display, and the source is shown. Preferentially, a gene symbol approved by the species-specific nomenclature committee is chosen (e.g. the appropriate HUGO HGNC symbol for human). Failing that, a UniProt/Swiss-Prot identifier, a NCBI RefSeq accession number or a UniProt/TrEMBL accession number is assigned.

  • Ensembl Gene ID - the Ensembl stable gene identifier.

  • Genomic Location

    • 'View gene in genomic location' indicates the chromosomal location of this gene and links to 'ContigView', which will only display the region corresponding to the gene.

    • 'This gene is located in sequence' will display the gene in 'ContigView' including its surrounding region.

    In either case a green background in the 'Detailed View' panel will highlight the gene.

  • Description - a short description of the gene. This is taken from the UniProt/Swiss-Prot entry the predicted gene is mapped to, or, if none available, from a NCBI RefSeq or UniProt/TrEMBL entry. If a predicted gene has not been mapped to any such entries, no description will be given. If its translation is however part of an Ensembl protein family cluster, the consensus annotation of that family may be informative. See the links to Ensembl 'FamilyView' in the 'Protein Family' section on Ensembl 'ProteinView' pages.

  • Prediction Method - A brief summary of the method used for the prediction of the gene set (Ensembl genes, EST genes).

  • Sequence Markup - This section allows you to view the underlying genomic sequence for this gene. While exons are highlighted by default, SNPs and flanking sequences can be optionally annotated.

  • Export Data - a link to Ensembl 'ExportView'. The 'ExportView' page will be automatically filled in with the gene information and provides a quick way to obtain the genomic sequence of the gene in plain FASTA format or annotated in EMBL or GenBank flat file format. Note that genomic sequence obtained from 'ExportView' is always from the 'forward' strand of the genome sequence assembly. Additional options for sequence export, including genome sequence export in the orientation of the annotated gene, can be found in 'EnsMart'.

  • SNP Information The 'View information about variations on this gene' links to Ensembl 'GeneSNPView' pages annotating single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) and Pfam domains along the genomic sequence underlying the particular gene model.

  • Transcript Structure - This section lists transcript predictions for this gene, with gene symbols and 'Ensembl Transcript IDs'. [Transcript information], [Exon information] and [Protein information] links provide more specialised information for each transcript on Ensembl 'TransView', 'ExonView', and 'ProteinView' pages, respectively. A summary of transcript and protein information for each transcript also appears lower down on the 'GeneView' page and can be reached by clicking on the transcript name.

    A diagram illustrates exon and intron structures for all Ensembl transcript models. This illustration can be further customised choosing manually curated 'Vega Transcripts', 'RNA Transcripts', 'GENSCAN predictions' and 'SNPs' from the 'Features' option in the golden menu bar.

    Any untranslated regions of the transcript (5' and 3' UTRs) are displayed as coloured outlines, while the predicted coding regions (CDS) are shown in solid colour. The black arrows indicate the size of the genomic region spanned by the transcript model.

  • Orthologue Prediction - a link to the closest putative orthologue genes in other species. Links are given between human, mouse and rat with all other species including chimp, chicken, zebrafish, Takifugu rubripes, Anopheles gambiae, Drosophila melanogaster, Caenorhabditis elegans and Caenorhabditis briggsae). A link at the bottom of this section leads to a corresponding Ensembl 'MultiContigView' page, which allows display of both genome sequence and annotation information for several species simultaneously. By default, all species with orthologues defined in this section are selected. Further species could be added or others dropped directly from 'MultiContigView' pages.

    The homologous genes represent the best reciprocal BLAST hits for the two species (Best Reciprocal Hit: BRH), with additional pairs obtained by a combination of BLAST and location information (Reciprocal Hit based on Synteny: RHS) for more closely related species. These homologues may therefore potentially represent orthologues.

    The chimp/human homologues are obtained from whole genome BLASTz alignments between human and chimp rather than by reciprocal BLASTP analysis and are designated DWGA (Derived from Whole Genome Alignments). This was also the method used to predict the chimp genes, by mapping the human genes onto the chimp chromosomes. See Description on the relevant Ensembl Gene Report.

    Clicking on the putative orthologue gene will take you to a 'GeneView' display of that gene within the web site for the other species. See also the 'SyntenyView' help page. Where no homology matches are shown, you may wish to explore the Ensembl protein family (see below).

    dN (number of non-synonymous substitutions / number of non-synonymous sites) and dS (number of synonymous substitutions / number of synonymous sites) values were generated using the codeml program included in the PAML package (1). With the parameters we have used, codeml performs pair wise Maximum Likelihood calculations of dN and dS for each set of orthologues. We have used the F3x4 codon evolution model (2). This takes into account both the bias deriving from the different probabilities of transition (T<->C and A<->G) versus transversion (T/C<->A/G) mutations, and the bias due to different nucleotide frequencies at the three codon positions.

    dN and dS values are only provided for orthologues from some species pairs, i.e. human/mouse, human/rat, and mouse/rat. Orthologues for other species pairs are too divergent for dS to be an accurate measure. Most synonymous sites will have be subjected to more than one mutation and ancestral changes cannot be reliably inferred from extant sequences, (i.e. dS is saturated).

    Orthologue predictions for human/mouse, human/rat and mouse/rat may not be perfect. Incorrect assignments will manifest anomalously high dS values. We have, therefore, applied a cut-off of twice the median value of all dS for each species pair as the criterion for displaying the dN/dS ratio. Predicted orthology relationships with dS above this threshold are likely to be errors.

    The following dS threshold values have been used: 

                       dS threshold

   human/mouse        1.26978
   human/rat          1.28408
   mouse/rat          0.40745

    Therefore, dN/dS ratios for human/mouse orthologues are only displayed when dS <= 1.26978, otherwise "--".

    NB: The dN, dS values for elegans/briggsae were calculated but dS values were considered as saturated (median = 2.26584, >> 1). After applying the dS threshold (4.53168), the median and average on the remaining set were 1.93962 and 2.062 respectively, and comparable with the data that was published in the Caenorhabditis briggsae genome paper (3), average 1.78 (no median was provided in the paper).

    References

    1. Yang, Z. (1997)
    "PAML: a program package for phylogenetic analysis by maximum likehood."
    Comput. Appl. Biosci. 1997 13: 555-556.

    2. Goldman, N. & Zang, Y. (1994)
    "A codon-based model of nucleotide substitution for protein-coding DNA sequences."
    Mol. Biol. Evol. 11, 725-736.

    3. Stein, LD et al. (2003)
    "The Genome Sequence of Caenorhabditis briggsae: A Platform for Comparative Genomics." PLoS Biol. 1, 166-192.

  • Paralogue Prediction - a link to putative paralogues.
    The identification of paralogues (recently duplicated human genes) has been conducted using a simple approach and it is only available for human at the moment.
    A raw set of gene homologies was derived from an all-versus-all BLAST approach against a dataset of Ensembl predicted genes including mouse and rat genes for out-groups. The resulting pairs were then filtered and ranked according to genetic distance and gene coverage. Groups of duplicated human genes were determined by clustering genes that shared common reciprocal matches. Hence, groups of recently duplicated genes identified in this manner have phylogenetic meaning and can be formally defined as being paralogous human genes that have arisen since the human-rodent divergence.

  • Disease Matches - genetic diseases associated with the gene. Identifiers link to OMIM gene locus entries, while [View disease information] links lead to more specialised information on Ensembl 'DiseaseView' pages. Note that OMIM entries in this section are those on the OMIM Morbid Map. Links to OMIM entries for many additional genes can be found in the 'Similarity Matches' sections of the 'Transcript/Translation Summary' tables.

  • GeneDAS Sources - 'GeneDAS' is a semantic extension to the DAS protocol that allows the exchange of annotations via gene or protein identifiers. Ensembl 'GeneView' acts as a client for this server, using the data as additional annotation for Ensembl predicted genes. Further 'GeneDAS' developments will allow users to display their own gene annotations.

    This implementation currently includes:

    • UniProt/Swiss-Prot literature references, which link to the NCBI PubMed literature database.

    • HUGO HGNC literature references. This set was kindly provided by the EBI text-mining group and provides an association of human HUGO HGNC gene symbols with NCBI PubMed literature references.

    • Genetic Association Database, which is an archive of human genetic association studies of complex diseases and disorders.

    • Reactome - a knowledgebase of biological processes

Transcript/Translation Summary

Below the 'Ensembl Gene Report' is a series of tables summarising each transcript and translation product predicted for this gene.

Each transcript table displays the following information.

  • Transcript Name A name is assigned to the transcript, matching entries in external databases. A more detailed description of the algorithm is in the Gene section above.

    Basic information such as the 'Ensembl Transcript ID', the number of exons, the transcript length, as well as the translation length are also provided in this section. [Transcript information], [Exon information] and [Protein information] links provide more specialised information for each transcript on Ensembl 'TransView', 'ExonView', and 'ProteinView' pages, respectively.

  • Similarity Matches - a list of links to external databases. Links to UniProt/Swiss-Prot, NCBI RefSeq and UniProt/TrEMBL are made on the basis of sequence similarity and also display identity percentages, calculated on the protein level.

    Target %id indicates the percentage of the external database sequence matching the Ensembl predicted sequence.

    Query %id indicates the percentage of the Ensembl predicted sequence matching the external database sequence.

    Other links are inferred from these mappings (e.g. NCBI LocusLink via RefSeq; Reactome (formerly Genome Knowledge Base, GKB), EMBL and Protein IDs via UniProt/Swiss-Prot).

    The [align] links lead to Ensembl 'AlignView' pages, which display an alignment of the Ensembl transcript prediction to the external database sequence.

    Ensembl establishes mappings to microarray probe set identifiers by matching probe set sequences to Ensembl transcripts. Mapping is successful if the probe set sequences match the transcript or an additional 2 kb down stream sequence window immediately adjacent the most 3' exon. For more detailed information see Datasets in EnsMart or Affymetrix probe to Ensembl transcript mapping.

  • GO - Gene Ontology terms represent a controlled vocabulary developed by the Gene Ontology Consortium. Ensembl associates GO terms via UniProt/Swiss-Prot mappings as outlined in the 'Similarity Matches' section above. Evidence codes are also shown and refer to the evidence used for the initial assignment of GO terms to UniProt/Swiss-Prot records. More than one evidence code may be shown where an Ensembl protein has been mapped to more than one UniProt/Swiss-Prot entry. Clicking on a particular identifier takes you to an Ensembl 'GOView' page displaying the context of the term in the ontology, as well as a list of other Ensembl genes mapped to the term. Clicking on the term itself will take you to a Gene Ontology search form. For more details on Ensembl Gene Ontology mapping, see the 'GOView' help page.

  • InterPro - a list of links to InterPro domains predicted for the translation product of this particular transcript. There is also an option [View other Ensembl genes with this domain] leading to Ensembl 'DomainView' pages.

  • Protein Family - a link to 'FamilyView', listing protein family members and annotating their chromosomal positions on a karyotype ideogram.

  • Transcript Structure - A diagram displaying exon and intron structures of the Ensembl transcript model. Any untranslated regions of the transcript (5' and 3' UTRs) are displayed as coloured outlines, while the predicted coding regions (CDS) are shown in solid colour. The black arrows indicate the size of the genomic region spanned by the transcript model. The small red arrow indicates the orientation of the transcript relative to the genome sequence in standard notation.

  • Protein Features shows a scale bar corresponding to the number of amino acids in the protein, together with bars representing the location of InterPro protein domain predictions. For more details see Ensembl 'ProteinView'.

Vega Gene Report

Vega genes represent a set of manually annotated genes. Finished genomic sequence is analysed on a clone-by-clone basis using a combination of similarity searches against DNA and protein databases, as well as a series of ab initio gene predictions (GENSCAN, GENEWISE). Gene structures are annotated on the basis of human interpretation of the combined supportive evidence generated during sequence analysis. In parallel, experimental methods are being applied to extend incomplete gene structures and discover new genes. The latter is initiated by comparative analysis of the finished sequence with vertebrate datasets such as the Riken mouse cDNAs, mouse whole-genome shotgun data and Genoscope Tetraodon nigrovirides evolutionarily conserved regions (ecores).

EST Gene Report

Generally, Ensembl gene builds are based on experimental evidence from UniProt/Swiss-Prot, NCBI RefSeq and UniProt/TrEMBL sequence records (Curwen et al., 2004). For all well-established species with sufficient amounts of supporting evidence Ensembl provides an additional EST gene gene-build solely based on a purified Expressed Sequence Tag set (Eyras et al., 2004). In keeping both gene-builds separate Ensembl accounts for the fact that the quality of EST information is quite heterogeneous. But on the other hand, ESTs can provide useful hints for the existence of further transcripts, especially alternatively spliced ones. Gene-builds for less characterised species are still based on supporting evidence from the public sequence databases. Since the amount of species-specific evidence for these species is generally too small to support a full gene-build, these builds are mainly based on information from closely related species. Ensembl may also directly exploit EST evidence for its gene-predictions in these cases. A separate EST gene set is in those cases not available.

More detailed information on Ensembl gene-builds and EST gene-builds is available from the publications section.

The search box at the top of the page allows you to search for any identifier present in Ensembl. For detailed instructions see the Ensembl 'TextView' page.
